About Us

"Whatsoever thy hand findeth to do, do it with thy might" - Ecclesiastes 9:10

We are the Tau Beta Chapter of Theta Tau, a professional national engineering fraternity for university students founded on on October 15, 1904 at the University of Minnesota in Minneapolis, Minnesota. We take pride in uniting all our members in pursuit of three primary goals:

Professional Development

The Tau Beta chapter of Theta Tau provides many opportunities for both networking and building skills that make for a great engineer, such as meals with professors, resumanias, and networking with alumni. Community Service

Theta Tau also gives back to the community, working with organizations such as Paper for Water and For the Love of the Lake and setting up community service events such as helping clean up White Rock Lake. Members complete 8 hours of community service each semester.

Brotherhood

Of course, what would a fraternity be without brotherhood? Members participate in events such as movie nights, arcades, and going to on-campus and members' other organizations' events in groups!

Our Purpose

The purpose of Theta Tau is to develop and maintain a high standard of professional interest among its members and to unite them in a strong bond of fraternal fellowship.
